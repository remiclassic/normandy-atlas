## Function before style

Historians stress adaptation: earthworks and timber, then stone keeps and curtain walls, varying by terrain, patron, and quarry. English motte-and-bailey patterns do not copy Sicily’s lava fortresses or the Levant’s massive enceintes—similar social functions, different masonries.

## Regions on this atlas

Compare England (London, Rochester), Wales (Chepstow), Sicily (Aci Castello, Erice), and the Crusader Levant (Krak, Kerak). The Norman Expansion layer highlights **sites**; it does not catalogue every phase of construction.
