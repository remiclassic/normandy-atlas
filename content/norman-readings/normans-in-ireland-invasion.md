## Leinster exiles and the crossing

The twelfth-century invasion is a political chain: Diarmait Mac Murchada’s exile, French-speaking lords in his pay, Henry II’s intervention, and the consolidation of an English lordship that would outlast any one adventurer. “Anglo-Norman” is the usual label—ethnic labels in the sources are messier than the shorthand.

## Ports and castles

Dublin, Wexford, Waterford, and Trim on the map correspond to landing places, river control, and administrative centers. Carrickfergus marks the Ulster bridgehead in a later phase. Read each pin with the Irish annals and Anglo-Norman records side by side.
