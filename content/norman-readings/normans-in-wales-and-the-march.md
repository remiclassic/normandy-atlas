## Marcher lordship

The Welsh frontier was not a straight line. Norman earls and barons built castles and pushed lordship along river valleys; Welsh rulers adapted, allied, and struck back. “Marcher” privilege—flexible jurisdiction—was as important as any single fortress.

## Chepstow, Cardiff, Pembroke

The atlas pins mark major nodes in that story: early stone at Chepstow, a Marcher seat at Cardiff, Pembroke as a springboard toward Ireland. None of these pins “explains” Wales; they orient you geographically while you read the chronicles and modern surveys.

## Historical caution

Avoid treating Welsh resistance as a static backdrop. Identity, alliance, and violence shifted decade by decade—exactly what a map cannot animate by itself.
