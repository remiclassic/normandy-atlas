## Surveys and starting points

- **Normandy:** David Bates, *Normandy Before 1066*; *William the Conqueror* (Yale English Monarchs). Lucien Musset and regional surveys for Scandinavian settlement and ecclesiastical networks.
- **England:** David C. Douglas, *William the Conqueror*; R. Allen Brown, *The Normans and the Norman Conquest*; Marjorie Chibnall, *Anglo-Norman England*; Hugh M. Thomas, *The English and the Normans*; specialist volumes on the north and Domesday.
- **France / Angevin:** John Gillingham, *The Angevin Empire*; Kate Norgate (older political narrative — supplement with current work on 1204 and Capetian administration).
- **Italy / Sicily:** Graham Loud, *The Age of Robert Guiscard*; Donald Matthew, *The Norman Kingdom of Sicily*; Jeremy Johns on Arabic administration and documentary culture; art-historical literature on Cefalù, Monreale, Palermo.
- **Crusades / Antioch:** Ralph-Johannes Lilie, *Byzantium and the Crusader States*; Thomas Asbridge on Antioch; *Cambridge History of the Crusades* for state of the field.
- **Wales / Ireland:** R. R. Davies, *Domination and Conquest*; Robin Frame, *Colonial Ireland, 1169–1369*; Seán Duffy and Tadhg O’Keeffe for high medieval Ireland.
- **Castles and military:** R. Allen Brown, Hugh Kennedy (*Crusader Castles*), and national corpora (English Heritage, French *castellologie*).

## How to use this list

This is a **starter shelf**, not an exhaustive library. For any pin on the map, prefer **recent monographs**, **corpus editions**, and **excavation reports** for that site; use the thematic essays in this hub for framing and comparison.

## Editions and databases

Charter volumes (national and regional), **Regesta** series, and digital finding aids will outperform any static list. When this atlas links a reading to a node, follow the node’s **further reading** lines for immediately relevant titles.
