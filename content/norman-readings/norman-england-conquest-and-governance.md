## 1066 and after

Hastings was decisive militarily, but “conquest” unfolded over years: castle-building, northern risings, the Harrying of the North (read in recent work as both campaign and narrative problem), and the slow knitting of a cross-Channel realm under William and his successors.

## Governance: sheriffs, shires, Domesday

The Domesday survey is a fiscal snapshot, not an ethnography. Castles in London, York, Norwich, and Warwick were instruments of lordship and administration as much as fortification. Treat each English pin as a **site of power** with its own documentary trail.

## Wales and Ireland in the same century

Norman lords in England were often the same families pushing into Wales and, later, Ireland. Compare this essay with [Normans in Wales and the March](/norman-readings/normans-in-wales-and-the-march) and [Anglo-Norman Ireland](/norman-readings/normans-in-ireland-invasion)—without flattening three distinct stories into one.
