## Angevin dukes, French kings

Henry II and his sons held Normandy as part of a larger Plantagenet bloc; Philip II’s campaigns culminated in 1204 with the French capture of key fortresses. Château Gaillard and Gisors on the map are material reminders of frontier competition, not just “Norman” architecture in isolation.

## Pirou and the Cotentin

Baronial castles such as Pirou illustrate local lordship within the duchy—different scale from royal siege engines on the Seine. Contextualize each pin with chronology: who held it, when, and under what crown.

## Language

“Angevin” and “Norman” are political labels for overlapping elites. This essay ties to [Normandy from grant to duchy](/norman-readings/normandy-from-grant-to-duchy) for earlier centuries and to [England](/norman-readings/norman-england-conquest-and-governance) for cross-Channel rule.
